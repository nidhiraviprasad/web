My Journey ━━━━━━━━━━━━

I first started programming when I joined my school's Girls Who Code club in sixth grade. I wrote my first "Hello, World" program in HTML in this club, and ended up creating my first website. The more I learned about programming, the more interested I became. I started learning JavaScript on free online websites, and started learning Java and Python as well. The Raspberry Pi sparked my interest; the idea of a credit card-sized computer fascinated me. Using the GPIO on the Pi taught me about circuitry and hardware and how enjoyable working with hardware can be (as I once thought hardware was boring and difficult to learn). I started algorithmic programming in 9th grade when I learned about the USACO contests. I enjoy the problem-solving aspect of programming, as it challenges me to come up with creative yet practical solutions. Although I find algorithmic programming very interesting, I never understood its applications and importance. This led me to start creating projects. I want to learn about how all the parts of a project come together to form one product. To understand this process, I want to create my own projects in this fashion. My journey with programming is far from over and I am very excited to share the rest of my journey!

All about my hobbies and interests!

Dance ━━━━━━━━━━━━

I have been learning Bharatanatyam [a form of Indian classical dance] since I was seven years old. I learn at Bayarea Nritya Gurukul from Guru Megha Shetty. My group and I have competed and won in several dance competitions. Currently, I have completed five out of seven exams to get a Bachelor of Arts in Bharatnatyam from a center in India [expected completion: December 2021]. I am training for my Arangetram [August 2020], a three-hour live solo performance.

Aside from Bharatnatyam, I also like to do hip-hop and hip-hop bollywood fusion dances. I have choreographed and participated in dances for my high school's homecoming, in addition to choreographing dances for family gatherings. I love to choreograph due to the endless creativity I can use as a choreographer.

Badminton ━━━━━━━━━━━━

I started learning badminton in fifth grade. I was drawn to the lightness and precision of the sport and enjoyed observing the various strategies players would use to defeat their opponents. I tried out for the school team and became a competing player in my freshman year. I was awarded with the Most Improved Player Award and the Scholar Athlete Award at the end of the season.

Jewelry Making ━━━━━━━━━━━━

Making jewelry has been one of my favorite hobbies since the fourth grade. I started by making simple bracelets with elastic and adding pendants onto chain. As I have grown and gained more experience, I have been creating necklaces and bracelets with more complicated beading patterns. One of my favorite things to do is work with wire. I have been using wire to create hoop earrings and charms, as well as rings and bracelets.

Baking ━━━━━━━━━━━━

I have had a long journey with baking, going from boxed cake mix to creating my own tres leches cake. It started as an excuse to be able to eat more desserts, but it is now a fun hobby I can do by myself, with friends, or with family. My favorite desserts that I have baked are a tres leches cake and a spiced apple cake!
